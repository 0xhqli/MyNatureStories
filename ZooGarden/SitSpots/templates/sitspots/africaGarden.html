Anna's Hummingbird

Male//photo by Kyle Blaney

female//photo by Ryan Winkleman

Description : Tiny green and gray bird, with a straight short bill and a fairly broad tail. Which extends longer than the wingtips.

Diet: Nectar from flowering plants, including currant and gooseberry are their main diet. They also can be found to eat a variety of insects, the smaller ones that can be found on a web or flower.

Habitat: Common in urban and suburban settings. Other habitats they can be found in are chaparral, costal scrubs and open woodlands.

Hooded Oriole

male//photo by Kevin Groeneweg

female//photo by Anne (Webster) Leight

Description : Same size as a robin, but these birds have a black and yellow-orange tints to them. They are shown to have long rounded tails and longish necks. Even though they are black and orange they still have bits of white that show on their wings.

Diet: With their curved small beaks they are able to go close to the ground and flip leaves over to find small insects, such as ants, grasshoppers, larvae and caterpillars. They also enjoy eating fruits and nectar from plants and flowers.

Habitat: Open, dry areas in the Southwest with scattered trees including cottonwoods and more.

Rufous - crowned Sparrow

photo by Doug Hitchcox

Description : Large sparrow with round heads and longer tails. They have a thick, pointed bill. Colors are usually brown with a small tint of orange.

Diet: Insects are their main diets in the spring and summer, while stems and seeds during winter. They scratch the floor looking for small insects.

Habitat: Dry open hillsides that are covered with grasses, rocks and scattered shrubs such as open chaparrals are where they are most common. They tend to be spotted in areas that have been burned in California.
