Acorn Woodpecker

photo by Charlie Morey

Description : Medium sized, black and white clown faced bird with a red crown, glossy black and white head. With one red or yellow feather.

Diet: Consists of insects, sap, oak, catkins, fruit and flower nectar. Sometimes grass seeds, lizards and bird eggs.

Habitat: Pine=Oak woodlands where oak trees are plentiful. Also found in riparian corridors and in Douglas firs, redwood and tropical hardwood forests as long as oaks are available and near by.

Terrestrial Biomes: forest; rainforest

California Scrub-Jay

photo by Brad Moon

Description : They have a blue forehead streaked with white eyebrows and a mixture of gray and black around their eyes. Breast, sides and bellies are gray or white and their mantle is brown or black.

Diet: Omnivorous, diet consists of a range of animal and plant material. Fruits such as cherries and Insects such as beetles are some of their main diet.

Habitat: Very dry, open hot coastal habitats, where human populations are dense. Lower elevations in thick grass, brush and low shrubs. Often found to be in woodlands with a dense environment.

Terrestrial Biomes: desert or dune; savanna

Bushtit

photo by Kevin Krebs

Tiny and kinglet-sized birds. They are pulmp with large heads and long tails but short with stubby bills. Colors range from brown, gray and black.

Diet: Small insects and spiuders, including the smallest ones that are living on leaves and twigs. They do eat plant material but its less common for them to do so.

Habitat: Open woods or scrubby areas, particularly pine-oak woodlands and chaparral. They also live in scrubs, sagebrush's and streamside woods.
